Nagpur

Nagpur is a large city in the central Indian state of Maharashtra. The 19th-century Nagpur Central Museum displays items found locally, including fossils, sarcophagi and Mughal weaponry. The Raman Science Centre has hands-on exhibits and a planetarium. Sitabuldi Fort, in the Sitabuldi Hills, was the site of an 1817 battle.

History of Nagpur

The city got its name from the River Nag or Nag people and is known since prehistoric times. Nagpur and its surrounding region also find a mention in the Vedic and Mauryan scriptures. Nagpur citys foundation was laid by the Gond King of Deogad "Bakht Buland Shah" in the year 1703. Chand Sultan, successor to Bakht Buland Shah, constructed a three-mile long wall around his City by the Nag River. In 1743, it became the capital of Raghoji Rao Bhonsles kingdom.

Culture of Nagpur

It is rich with cultural and literary societies which includes Vidarbha Sahitya Sangh (which is used for developing Marathi), Vidarbha Hindi Sahitya Sammelan (for promoting Hindi), Vidarbha Rashtrabhasha Prachar Samiti (used for spreading and promoting Hindi) and many more. The Marathi Sahitya Sammelan, were conducted twice at Nagpur, which helped in empowering the Marathi Literature.
